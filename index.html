About Nelson Mandela

Nelson Mandela (1918-2013) was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist who served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election.

Major Achievements

  • Leader of the anti-apartheid movement
  • Nobel Peace Prize winner (1993)
  • South Africa's first black president
  • Established the Truth and Reconciliation Commission

His Legacy

Mandela's legacy lives on as a symbol of peace, resilience, and equality. He fought for a democratic and free society where all people could live together in harmony and equal opportunity.
